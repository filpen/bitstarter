Seatbelt

Develop together, anywhere, for free.

Find a remote pair programming partner.

Do you need help with a programming task and nobody but your cat is around? Would you like to start contributing to open source but the task looks overwhelming? Do you feel like helping or mentoring a developer?

Seatbelt is the meeting point for developers looking for help and offering support using pair programming. Schedule a meeting and start hacking together with your partner!

How it works

  1. 1. Post a request for help, or navigate through available requests.

  2. 2. Got an offer or found a request? Schedule a meeting with your partner.

  3. 3. Hack together on the task, learn and have fun!

Grow

You have the experience of another developer at your disposal: use it wisely. Even if you pair during your day job, pairing with people with a completely different background on the same technology is a very different learning experience.

Get involved

Not everyone lives near a tech hub. You are not stimulated enough to write in your blog every day. Heck, you don't even have a blog! Get in touch with other developers, hack together and make development fun again.

Code review

Having another pair of eyes looking over the code helps you prevent making bad design decisions early on. The earlier issues are discovered, the better it is: that is why instant code reviews are much more efficient than offline reviews.

Learn

You are a master of .NET? That's great! Come and play with JavaScript, RoR, Scala, Clojure... Make yourself uncomfortable. Find an experienced developer to help you to get started the right way!

Got a problem?

If you are stuck and stackoverflow keeps closing your questions because they are too broad, find somebody to help you out. Sometimes you just need a little bit of input from somebody who understands what you are trying to accomplish.

Tutorials

Seatbelt starts where the tutorials end. You just finished reading a book about a cool tech, now you stare at an empty editor wondering where to begin. Grab an experienced developer and start hacking together!
