Seatbelt

Announcing a new way to improve your development skills: together, anywhere, for free.

Get and give help with software development tasks, remotely, via virtual pair programming.

Spread the word!

Do you need help with a programming task and nobody but your cat is around? Would you like to start contributing to open source but the task looks overwhelming? Do you feel like helping or mentoring a developer?

Seatbelt is the meeting point for developers looking for help and offering support using pair programming. Schedule a meeting and start hacking together with your partner!

How it works

  1. 1. Post a request for help, or navigate through available requests.

  2. 2. Got an offer or found a request? Schedule a meeting with your partner.

  3. 3. Hack together on the task, learn and have fun!

Thank you, you are awesome! We will launch very soon and we will get back at you as soon as we do. In the meanwhile, help us spread the word!

The indifference bubble

If you don't live close to a major high-tech hub, you know the problem. You read books and blog posts and are excited about new ideas. You go to as many conferences as you can. If you are lucky, you might even attend courses taught by very talented people. But you feel isolated.

Not everyone around you is a software engineering geek. They are not fascinated by the fact that you can run JavaScript on the server and its implications. That you can write the same thing much more efficiently with the right language. They do not see it. They are not interested. And you know what? That is perfectly fine. People get off on different things.

But you want more. And you are not alone. There are other people like you around the world, but they are also trapped in an indifference bubble. They want out. You want out. Fasten your seat belt, because you are about to break out of the bubble. Together.
